ICT Cube League (2013-present)

Backstory

In the summer of 2013, a podcast called The Magic Box (sadly now podfaded) released an episode wherein they described a Magic the Gathering league centered around a format called Cube.

I had been curating my own Cube for a few months at this point and wanted more opportunities to play the game with others. I saw starting the league as a chance to create a playgroup and get a lot of feedback about the cube itself.

I reached out to Hero Complex Games, a store where I have played since it opened in 2011. They had an event on Tuesday nights that was not drawing the attendance they wanted, so they offered to host the league.

Today

Over three years and fourteen seasons later, ICT Cube League is going strong. We've hosted hundreds of games and given out over $1,000 in prizes.

The cube can be viewed and sampled at CubeTutor.com. I am currently developing a Laravel site for the league as a promotional tool and a portfolio piece.
