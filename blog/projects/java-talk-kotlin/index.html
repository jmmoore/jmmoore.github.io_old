devICT Java Talk (August 2016)

Backstory

In the summer of 2016, Gregg Bolinger had the desire to start a series of tech talks specifically centered around Java and its offshoots. He put out the call for talks on the devICT Slack. At the time I was employed as a Developer/DevOps Engineer at a company that primarily used Java as its language of choice.

Leading up to this point I had been experimenting with other languages on the JVM like Clojure and Scala. I don't remember exactly how I discovered Kotlin, but its clean syntax and the way it handles null pointer exceptions made me a fan immediately. I informed Gregg that I'd be more than willing to give a talk about Kotlin, and he assented enthusiastically.

Stage Fright

I'd never given a tech talk before. Despite that, I had a lot of experience speaking in front of different groups of people. For the better part of a year I was the MC for Wichita's (at the time) only Poetry Slam, as well as a performer and regular at their open mic nights.

I had the tremendous opportunity to open for the Night Kite Revival when they performed in Wichita, and read a few poems between sets for Buddy Wakefield's Wichita performance.

Given that, I spent the majority of my time on the slides and making the content accurate, interesting, and informative. I watched several Kotlin talks, mostly from developers of the language, and cribbed notes liberally.

The Talk

It went off mostly without a hitch! My slides were made using reveal.js and hosted on my personal site. You can take a look at them here. There is not video of me physically giving the talk as there wasn't a camera present, but you can listen to the talk and read the slides on Youtube. I also made available on github my notes, research and a PDF of the slides.

Takeaways

I really liked giving this presentation. I spent a significant amount of time and energy researching it and still wish that I could have done better. It made me want to write and speak and teach about technical things for a living.

Expect more from me in this vein soon.
